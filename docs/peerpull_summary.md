# PeerPull
## Hackathon Concept Summary & PRD Foundation
*Prepared: March 2026 | 7-Day Hackathon Starting Tomorrow*

---

## 1. Product Overview

PeerPull is a peer-to-peer feedback exchange platform built for indie builders, founders, and makers. The core mechanic is a 2-for-1 credit exchange: you must give a quality review to earn the right to receive one. This ensures every reviewer has skin in the game, and every piece of feedback is credible, useful, and comes from someone who understands what it means to build something.

---

## 2. The Problem Being Solved

Founders face a persistent and frustrating feedback problem:

- Friends and family give fake encouragement rather than honest critique
- Agency or professional feedback is expensive and slow
- Generic feedback platforms have no incentive structure for quality
- Most reviewers are lurkers with no stake in being genuinely helpful

---

## 3. Core Value Proposition

**Tagline (working):**

> "Real feedback from real builders. Finally."

**Project Description (for hackathon submission):**

> Bad feedback kills good products. PeerPull is a structured peer exchange platform where indie builders, founders, and makers trade in-depth product feedback using a credit-based system that rewards quality over quantity. Because every reviewer is also a builder looking for feedback themselves, the incentive to be genuinely helpful is built into the model.

---

## 4. Core Mechanic: The 2-for-1 Exchange

The fundamental loop:

1. User gives a quality review to another builder's product
2. This earns them a review credit
3. They spend that credit to receive a review on their own product
4. The cycle repeats, building reputation and improving the product

### Cold Start Problem Solution

The 2-for-1 model inherently solves the cold start problem that kills most feedback platforms. Users cannot lurk passively. Every user who wants to receive must first contribute, which means from day one every user is both a consumer and a content producer.

---

## 5. Account Types

Three distinct account types with different motivations and use cases:

### Builder
- Primary user: indie hackers, solo founders, product makers
- Goal: receive quality feedback and build credibility as a thoughtful reviewer
- Profile grows based on review quality and history

### Investor
- Goal: deal flow and signal on promising early-stage products
- Access to a curated stream of vetted, actively developed products

### Early Adopter
- Goal: discover what is worth using before it goes mainstream
- Product hunting and early access motivation

---

## 6. Profile & Reputation System

The profile system is identified as a key differentiator and potential growth driver:

- Profiles grow in strength and visibility based on review quality and volume
- A strong profile (e.g. Level 8 Builder, 200 quality reviews) carries real social weight in the indie founder community
- Profile reputation unlocks additional benefits (to be defined in future iterations)
- Profiles become shareable assets that users want to display publicly

### Open Design Question: Review Quality Scoring

How review quality is measured needs to be defined. Options discussed include AI scoring of review depth and specificity, peer voting on reviews, or a combination. This is critical because without a quality gate the token economy can be gamed with low-effort one-line reviews.

---

## 7. Token Economy (Phase 2 Vision)

A cryptocurrency token layer is planned for a future phase. Key design decisions already made:

- Tokens are earned by giving reviews, not by paying
- A marketplace will allow users to buy tokens if they want feedback without doing reviews first
- Token pricing will be dynamic, based on supply and demand
- The economy self-regulates: high demand for feedback raises token price, incentivising more reviews

### Important Consideration

The crypto layer adds regulatory, technical, and perception complexity. Some founders will be energised by it, others will be sceptical. Decision: keep the token roadmap visible and transparent on the platform but do not build it in the hackathon. Tease the vision, execute the core loop first.

- **Secondary:** investors looking for deal flow from the indie community

## 10. Key Design Decisions & Open Considerations

### Decisions Made

- The 2-for-1 exchange is the non-negotiable core mechanic. No passive participation.
- Three account types from launch: Builder, Investor, Early Adopter
- Token economy is Phase 2, not Phase 1. Build the loop first.
- Hackathon audience is the ideal first user base: motivated, technical, understand the pain point
- Build-in-public approach: document the launch journey as a growth strategy

### Open Considerations

- How is review quality scored? AI, peer voting, or hybrid? This must be solved before the token economy is viable.
- What specific benefits does a stronger profile unlock? Needs to be defined to make the reputation loop motivating.
- What does the first complete exchange loop look like step by step? This is the core user story that needs to be the first thing built.
- How does moderation work? Who handles disputes about review quality or fraudulent activity?

---

## 11. Competitive Positioning

PeerPull is differentiated on two dimensions that no existing platform combines:

- **Enforced reciprocity:** unlike ProductHunt, Reddit, or Discord communities, you cannot receive without contributing
- **Builder-to-builder credibility:** unlike paid feedback services, reviewers are peers with direct experience of the same challenges
